Syrian Civil War: The Numbers

'A peaceful uprising against the president of Syria seven years ago has turned into a full-scale civil war. The conflict has left more than 350,000 people dead, devastated cities and drawn in other countries.'

The above chart displays the death tolls of innocent people killed in Syria from 2014 to 2017. The red line displays 2017, the orange line represents 2016, 2015 is the yellow line and 2014 is the brown line.

We hear so much about the Syrian Civil War crisis and about how people from Syria try to flee to the safety of other countries, which in itself has plenty of difficulties.

This second data visual shows Trend of Registered Syrian Refugees (in-camp) from all the way back in January 2013 with the number gradually rising toward the 600,000 thousand mark.

Here we take a look at the number of refugees taken from several different European countries. These numbers are taken from 2015, while I still plan to work on the newer numbers, as of right now they are seemingly disputed. Nonetheless, we hear quite a lot about this story on the news but seeing the visuals makes it much easier to digest.

"By visualizing information, we turn it into a landscape that you can explore with your eyes..." - D. McCandless
